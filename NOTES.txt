3. Rabies Prevention

What is Rabies?
Rabies is a deadly disease that can affect the brain and nervous system. Early symptoms include fever and headache, and as the disease progresses, it can cause confusion, paralysis, and even death.

How Rabies is Spread
Rabies is mainly spread through the saliva of infected animals, often through bites. The animals most likely to carry rabies are dogs, bats, raccoons, and other wild animals.

How to Protect Yourself
Vaccination is the best way to protect yourself and your pets from rabies. Make sure your pets get their rabies shots on time, and avoid interacting with wild animals.

If You’re Bitten
If you get bitten by an animal, wash the wound with soap and water, and go to a doctor right away. You may need a rabies shot, and it’s important to start treatment quickly.

4. Animal Bite Awareness

Different Types of Animal Bites
Animal bites can come from pets like dogs and cats or wild animals like raccoons and bats. Some animals carry more risk, so it’s important to know which ones to be cautious around.
First Aid for Animal Bites
- Clean the bite with soap and water.
- Apply pressure to stop any bleeding.
- Use an antiseptic and cover the bite with a clean bandage.
- See a doctor, especially if the animal that bit you was acting strange or is wild.
- 
Reporting Animal Bites
If you get bitten, report it to local authorities or animal control. They may need to quarantine the animal to check for rabies, and they can help you with next steps.
Preventing Animal Bites
Train pets to be well-behaved and always supervise kids around animals. Teach children to approach animals carefully, and avoid contact with wild animals whenever possible.


5. FAQs
General Questions

What should I do if I’m bitten by an animal?
Wash the wound right away with soap and water, apply pressure to stop bleeding, and get medical help as soon as possible.

How do I know if an animal has rabies?
Rabid animals may act strange, like being unusually aggressive or acting disoriented. If you're unsure, seek help from a professional.

Rabies Vaccination
How effective are rabies vaccines?
Rabies vaccines are very effective if given before or shortly after exposure to the virus.
Do all animals need rabies vaccines?
Yes! All dogs and cats should be vaccinated against rabies, and other pets may need it too depending on where you live.

Animal Bite Treatment
What should I do after a dog bite?
Follow the same first-aid steps: clean the wound, stop the bleeding, and get medical care. Rabies shots might be necessary, especially if the dog’s rabies status is unclear.
